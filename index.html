The Theorem Blackboard

Seven functions, each holding a small mathematical truth. Drag, slide, and play — every panel proves its property live, and signs off with ∎ when the math checks out. This is what tested by theorems feels like from the outside.

audio geometricLerp(220, 440, t)

One octave (220 → 440 Hz) split into twelve equal ratios is the chromatic scale. Each step multiplies the pitch by the same factor — the twelfth root of two. Press the keys.

robotics deadzone(v, t)

Inside the threshold the stick reads zero; past it, the output is rescaled so it still reaches full tilt — no jump at the edge. Drag the handle.

easing signedPow(x, k)

A response curve that keeps its sign through any exponent — push left, stay left. It solves the (-x)k = NaN trap and stays an odd, invertible function.

money proportionalSplit(total, …)

Split a bill into whole cents that reconcile to the exact total — never 33.33 + 33.33 + 33.33 = 99.99. The leftover cent goes to the largest remainder.

probability normalizeToSum(weights, 100)

Any set of weights, rescaled to add up to exactly 100% — the proportions between them never change. Slide the weights.

time series cumulativeSum(…deltas)

Running totals turn a list of changes into a balance over time. Add deposits and withdrawals — the last bar is always the grand total.

science roundToSignificant(n, figs)

Rounding by significant figures, not decimal places — the same setting works for a galaxy's mass and an atom's radius, because it follows the magnitude, not the decimal point.
